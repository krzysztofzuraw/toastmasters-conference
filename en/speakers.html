Simon Bucknall

An award-winning, international speaker, Simon helps high-achieving professionals to achieve greater impact through the spoken word.

In 2017, Simon represented Europe in the Final of the World Championship of Public Speaking in Vancouver, Canada, placing 2nd out of 30,000+ competing speakers from all over the world.

Over the past decade, Simon has helped professionals from more than 100 countries and from all sectors - including FTSE 100 & Fortune 100 executives, entrepreneurs, politicians, MBAs and academics as well as leaders in the not-for-profit sector.

With experience coaching C-suite executives at the highest level, Simon’s clients include General Electric, HgCapital, Rede Partners, TrendMicro, DPD, London Business School, Oxford University’s Blavatnik School of Government, Cambridge University’s Laing O’Rourke Centre for Construction Engineering & Technology and the Royal Academy of Engineering.

Simon’s career background spans executive headhunting, blue-chip corporate brand strategy consulting plus a stint working in the House of Commons as Head of Office for a senior Parliamentarian. He has featured as an expert commentator on BBC World News, BBC London Radio, Channel 5 News and on international broadcast radio networks around the world.

Simon holds degrees from Oxford University and the London School of Oriental & African Studies. He is a Faculty member at Oxford University’s Blavatnik School of Government and a Visiting Fellow in Professional Skills at Cambridge University.

In 2013, he was awarded the Freedom of the City of London.

Aleksandra Lewandowska

Fun, pun and energy provider. A storyshower with a linguistic and acting background, passionate about living from the heart and reminding people of their worth.

Translates books, does voice overs and emcees at events. The current District 95 Champion in Humorous Speeches and 3rd place winner at D95 World Championships of Public Speaking quarterfinals in Berlin 2017, serves as Vice President Education and Master Mentor at Toruń Toastmasters.

She believes that a good speech is a meaningful conversation with your audience. And she really loves to talk.

Peter Zinn

Peter won the European speech contest standing on a table and blowing down houses. A Toastmaster since 2007, he has been seen on district conferences quite a lot: as a contestant, as contest chair, as workshop leader. His focus is on the interaction between speaker and audience. That is where the magic happens. In 2016 he quit his successful job as cyber cop to become a professional speaker.
