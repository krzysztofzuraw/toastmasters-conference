Speakers

Simon Bucknall

An award-winning, international speaker, Simon helps high- achieving professionals to achieve greater impact through the spoken word.

In 2017, Simon represented Europe in the Final of the World Championship of Public Speaking in Vancouver, Canada, placing 2 nd out of 30,000+ competing speakers from all over the world.

Over the past decade, Simon has helped professionals from more than 100 countries and from all sectors - including FTSE 100 & Fortune 100 executives, entrepreneurs, politicians, MBAs and academics as well as leaders in the not-for- profit sector.

Aleksandra Lewandowska

Fun, pun and energy provider. A storyshower with a linguistic and acting background, passionate about living from the heart and reminding people of their worth.

Translates books, does voice overs and emcees at events. The current District 95 Champion in Humorous Speeches and 3rd place winner at D95 World Championships of Public Speaking quarterfinals in Berlin 2017, serves as Vice President Education and Master Mentor at Toruń Toastmasters.

She believes that a good speech is a meaningful conversation with your audience. And she really loves to talk.

Peter Zinn

Peter won the European speech contest standing on a table and blowing down houses. A Toastmaster since 2007, he has been seen on district conferences quite a lot: as a contestant, as contest chair, as workshop leader. His focus is on the interaction between speaker and audience. That is where the magic happens. In 2016 he quit his successful job as cyber cop to become a professional speaker.

Piotr Chimko

Manager with eighteen years of experience in IT, finance and procurement in large, multinational corporations. For more than three years, as business process manager, has contributed to global transformation of Philips. Passionate about effective communication and leadership.

Toastmaster since 2009, currently Program Quality Director responsible for education in District 95, which covers 18 countries in central, eastern and northern Europe.

Lukas Liebich

Career-wise: Project & Program Manager

Crazy about: Toastmasters. Since July 2017 he’s working as Club Growth Director for future District 110 (Wild East of Europe). Supporting Division Directors from Czechia to Croatia and Ukraine in achieving their Membership and Club growth targets.

Special skill: Reading books. Slowly.

Personal weakness: Sometimes takes himself too seriously. He’s fighting this terrible trait with support of his closest friends who don’t (take him too seriously). And by reading wise books.

What makes people nervous in his presence: He takes notes of brilliant / awkward things they say in his little black journal.

Adam Pioch

Founder of Toastmasters club in Poznan and Pila. He inspired dozens of people by public speaking, resulting in many successes and achievements of Toastmasters clubs in Poznan and development of clubs in other cities.

He specializes in one of the most demanding manuals in Toastmasters, i.e. Storytelling. It involves impersonating various characters, presenting dialogues, as well as intricate weaving of the dramaturgy of the story – what Adam additionally be speckles with a big dose of terror in a pure form. In everyday life a coach, trainer and mentor of many people engaged in writing and internet marketing. Teaches “public speaking” on MBA studies. A coach in the subject of websites auditing and storytelling.

Kacper Biłozor

Orator, mentor, trainer, toastmaster, and public speaking enthusiast.

He specializes in preparing speeches, presentations and public appearances, designing scrupulous speech structures, managing the stage and body language, copy-editing, rhetorical devices, text interpretation, and performative reading. He spoke at the Column Hall of the Polish Parliament in Warsaw and at the European Parliament Hemicycle in Brussels.

First place in the national impromptu speaking contest, second place in the national speech evaluation contest (public, critical, and motivating assessment), two-time semi-finalist and three-time finalist of the District 95 speech championships, in four different categories. A year after winning the national impromptu speaking contest, his protégé got to second place in the same category.

Przemysław Kutnyj

Trainer, consultant, spokesperson for ski station Dwie Doliny Muszyna – Wierchomla, connected with public relations business for 12 years.

He worked for companies such as Air France – KLM, Haribo, Sante, Motorola or PKO BP. Graduate from Polish studies at University of Warsaw, major in editing-publishing, postgraduate studies of Public Relations at Warsaw School of Economics and Business Coach College NOVO.

Involved in training business since 2007. His passions are public speaking, photography and history of Poland. In 2016 he became vice-champion of Poland in public speaking, and in January 2017 - champion of Poland in humorous speeches.

He writes fairy tales, co-creating blog Baju-baju.pl.

Vera Orac

Vera Orac is currently the L2 Area Director, and previously served as club president and VPE. A Toastmaster for over 10 years, she reached district level in the International Speech, Humorous Speech, Table Topics and Evaluation contests. When not competing, she has twice been the contest master for the district Humorous Speech contest finals. Vera has given workshops at district conferences, club officer trainings, the Czech clubs’ residential weekend, and of course, in clubs. Outside Toastmasters, she dabbles in improv theater and stand-up comedy.

Marek Zarzycki

DTM. Former president of Toastmasters Krakow and Division B Director. Economist, he has cooperated for 8 years with the largest companies in Poland, mainly in a field of consulting and analytics – currently as Strategic Planning Analyst. Trainer in creating and giving effective business presentations.

Company owner: zaPREZENTUJsię, and blogger: marekzarzycki.com.

Dawid Jary

Member of Wroclove Toastmasters and 1st Million Toastmasters clubs, twice Vice-President of Education of 1st Million Toastmasters, former secretary and sargeant of Wroclove Toastmasters, two times achieved Triple Crown award, coordinator of Young Leadership program, coordinator of Toastmasters Movie Academy, speechwriter at Volvo Polska, achieved Advanced Communicator Gold award, Polish champion in impromptu speeches, Polish vice-champion in humorous speeches.

Franciszek Szych

Energetic professional trainer, IT specialist, listener and smiler. 11 years in Toastmasters, past Persident and Area Director. Happiest while leading very interactive workshops on communication, or feedback. As Chief Pathways Ambassador, he helps implement Pathways in our future District 108. Loves mango, Spotify, long difficult conversations and his wife.

Magdalena Kędzia

She is a Toastmaster since Dec 2012. She obtained DTM title in Dec 2015. She provided feedback to Toastmasters as a mentor, coach, club sponsor and Area E1 governor. Currently she is a Pathways Ambassador and Vice-President of Education at Klub Zaawansowanych Mówców Roastmasters Bydgoszcz. In 2018 in Opole she achieved 3rd place in Polish championships in improvised speeches. Certified FRIS ® trainer and partner, certified mentor, coach and trainer of MaxieDISC consultants, individual and group coach (VCC certification) and coaching mentor (over 400h of individual sessions). Member of International Mentors Association. She owns a training and advisory company. For many years, she has been connected with sale of financial products. She specializes in trainings in the area of interpersonal communication, sale, management, public speaking, coaching and mentoring. You can learn about opinions of her customers at http://simmenadzer.pl/

Paweł Bochnowski

Expert in the field of change implementation and project management, who loves to share concrete and proven tools during workshops. In everyday life Paweł develops his experience and skills in the field of management (of teams, projects and processes) and implementation of large changes in large international organizations from finance and power industry. At the same time he shares practical knowledge he obtained there by cooperating with local companies and start-ups. He is heavily involved in development of project management community, currently he acts within Project Management Institute and Toastmasters International. In his work he believes in the principle of Continuous Improvement, therefore in his trainings he shares current trends from the most important conferences in Europe, which he verified in action in his projects and teams. He spends his free time, when he finally finds it, on lacrosse matches of Kosynierzy Wrocław, often in a role of master of ceremony. He also had the pleasure to comment all lacrosse matches during World Games 2017.
